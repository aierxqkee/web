The Tutberidze Figure Skating Saga


Yulia Lipnitskaya is the youngest Olympic single champion in the history of figure skating, having won the title in the team tournament at the age of 15 years 249 days.

Yulia Lipnitskaya, after winning the team competition, also became the first single figure skater in the history of Russia and the USSR to win the title of Olympic champion. She is also the youngest winner of the European Championships in the history of figure skating.


The first single figure skater in the history of Russia and the second in world history (after Kim Yong Ah), who won all the titles in world figure skating, the youngest figure skater who managed to win all the main starts.

Alina Zagitova is the first junior to score more than 70 points in the short program and 200 points in the sum of the programs; the first and only figure skater to overcome the threshold of 82 points in the short program according to the old judging system and the first figure skater to score 80 points in the short program and more than 150 in the free program according to the new one. She once broke the world record in the short program under the old (2004-2018) judging system and four times under the new one (2018 onwards).


According to the previous rating system, Evgenia Medvedeva is the first figure skater in the history of women's figure skating who scored more than 160 points in the free program, more than 80 points in the short program, and more than 240 points in total. The owner of historical world records in the free program and in the sum of points. She updated the world records in short and free programs 4 times, according to the sum of points - 3 times. She broke the world records of Kim Yong Ah and Mao Asada.


Anna Shcherbakova is the first senior woman to land a quad Lutz. Additionally, in 2019 she became the first woman to land two quad Lutz jumps in a program, with one done in combination with a triple toe loop. Shcherbakova has also landed a quad flip in domestic competition at the 2019 Russian Championships.


Alena Kostornaia currently holds the world record for the highest junior short program score in women's skating (22 November 2019, 85.04, 2019 NHK Trophy).


Trusova is the first female skater to land the quad Lutz, quad flip, and quad toe loop jumps, the first to land two and three ratified quads in a free skate. She currently holds four Guinness World Records; the fourth in recognition of her landed quadruple flip. Her technical score of 92.35 points was the highest ever recorded in women's singles skating at the junior and senior level until the GOE (Grade of Execution) system was changed at the end of the 2017–18 season.

She set another record by becoming the first female skater to land a quadruple jump in combination, a quad toe loop, and a triple toe loop. Trusova is the first and currently only female skater competing with four different types of quadruple jumps—toe loop, Salchow, flip, and Lutz. She is also the first female skater in history to score above 100 in technical elements.


Kamila Valieva is the current world record holder for the women's short program, free skating, and total scores. She has set nine world records during her career. She is the first woman skater to break the 250-, 260- and 270- point thresholds in the total score, within one season, the first to break the 170- and 180- point thresholds in the free skate, and the first to break the 90- point thresholds in the short program.
